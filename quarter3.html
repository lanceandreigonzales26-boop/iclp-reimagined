Topic 1

Percentage Composition

The simplest way to express concentration — what fraction of the mixture is the solute, expressed as a percentage.

01 / MASS PERCENT

Mass Percent (% m/m)

The mass of the solute divided by the total mass of the solution, multiplied by 100.

% m/m = (mass of solute / mass of solution) × 100

Example: 10 g NaCl dissolved in 90 g of water:
% = (10 / 100) × 100 = 10%

02 / VOLUME PERCENT

Volume Percent (% v/v)

Used when both solute and solvent are liquids. The volume of solute per total volume of solution.

% v/v = (volume of solute / volume of solution) × 100

Example: 40 mL ethanol in 100 mL solution:
% = (40 / 100) × 100 = 40%

03 / PERCENT IN COMPOUNDS

Percent Composition of a Compound

The percentage of each element's mass in a pure compound. Used to verify molecular formulas.

% element = (molar mass of element in formula / molar mass of compound) × 100

Example: % O in H₂O = (16.00 / 18.02) × 100 = 88.8%

% Mass

% = (mₛₒₗᵤₜₑ / mₛₒₗᵤₜᵢₒₙ) × 100

Both measured in grams

% Volume

% = (Vₛₒₗᵤₜₑ / Vₛₒₗᵤₜᵢₒₙ) × 100

Both measured in mL or L

% Element in Compound

% = (n × Mₑₗₑₘₑₙₜ / Mₘₒₗₑcᵤₗₑ) × 100

n = number of atoms of element

Topic 2

Molarity

The most commonly used unit of concentration in chemistry — moles of solute per liter of solution.

01 / DEFINITION

Molarity (M)

Molarity expresses how many moles of solute are present per liter of solution. It is temperature-dependent because volume changes with temperature.

M = moles of solute / liters of solution

A 1 M (1 molar) solution contains 1 mol of solute in every 1 L of solution.

02 / WORKED EXAMPLE

Solving for Molarity

Problem: What is the molarity of a solution made by dissolving 58.5 g of NaCl (M = 58.5 g/mol) in 500 mL of water?

Step 1: moles NaCl = 58.5 g ÷ 58.5 g/mol = 1.00 mol
Step 2: Convert 500 mL → 0.500 L
Step 3: M = 1.00 mol / 0.500 L = 2.00 M

03 / DILUTION

Dilution Formula

When a concentrated solution is diluted, moles of solute remain constant. This gives the dilution equation:

M₁V₁ = M₂V₂

Where M₁ and V₁ are the initial molarity and volume, and M₂ and V₂ are the final values after dilution.

Molarity

M = n / V

n in mol, V in liters

Moles from grams

n = mass (g) / molar mass (g/mol)

Look up molar mass on periodic table

Dilution

M₁V₁ = M₂V₂

Moles of solute are conserved

Topic 3

Molality & Other Units

Molality is concentration relative to the mass of solvent — useful for colligative properties because it is temperature-independent.

01 / MOLALITY

Molality (m)

Molality is the number of moles of solute per kilogram of solvent (not solution).

m = moles of solute / kg of solvent

Example: 2 mol NaCl dissolved in 1 kg of water = 2 m solution. Because it uses mass (not volume), molality does not change with temperature.

02 / M vs m

Molarity vs. Molality

Molarity (M) — moles per liter of solution. More practical for lab use. Changes with temperature.

Molality (m) — moles per kg of solvent. Temperature-independent. Used for boiling point elevation and freezing point depression calculations.

03 / ppm & ppb

Parts per Million (ppm)

Used for very dilute solutions (environmental, water quality). 1 ppm = 1 mg solute per 1 L of solution (approximately 1 mg/kg for water).

ppm = (mass of solute / mass of solution) × 10⁶

ppb (parts per billion) = × 10⁹. Common in water quality testing and toxicology.

Molality

m = n / kg(solvent)

Note: kg of solvent, not solution

ppm

ppm = (mₛₒₗᵤₜₑ / mₛₒₗᵤₜᵢₒₙ) × 10⁶
